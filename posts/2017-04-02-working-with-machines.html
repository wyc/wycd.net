title: Working With Machines tags: business, software —

R. David Dixon Jr. writes:

…the humans and machines work together side by side as tools in a large and distributed production process of specialized tasks. Neither the machine nor the human directly owns their time, but must bow to the demands of coordination.

Time ownership is a curious way to view relationships between machines and people, and among their own groups as well. When I ask the compiler to produce my program, am I a servant to it as I wait? What about heating up something in the microwave for 30 seconds? Most annoying are events too short in duration to defer checking of results, yet long enough to interrupt user concentration and flow.
